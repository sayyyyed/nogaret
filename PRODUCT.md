# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is employers, collaborators, and public readers evaluating Amir's software work, research interests, and technical judgment. Visitors may be scanning the portfolio quickly or reading deeply across projects, research notes, labs, and long-form writing.

## Product Purpose

This site is a personal website and portfolio that presents a long-term knowledge hub for software engineering, AI, educational systems, Arabic language technology, research notes, and experiments. Success means visitors can quickly understand the author's focus areas, inspect credible work, and follow the thinking behind projects over time.

## Brand Personality

Technical, scholarly, unique. The voice should feel precise and research-minded without becoming dry, generic, or template-like.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI landing-page patterns, SaaS hero templates, repeated badge-and-card scaffolds, decorative gradients without purpose, and any layout that feels like a generated portfolio theme. Avoid visual choices that make the site look interchangeable with other developer homepages.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with substance: writing, systems, research, and projects should carry the page.
2. Make expertise inspectable: visitors should see concrete topics, tools, and artifacts instead of vague claims.
3. Preserve a workbench feeling: the site should feel active, iterative, and alive without looking unfinished.
4. Prefer quiet distinctiveness: the design should be memorable through composition, typography, and pacing, not decoration.
5. Keep long-term maintainability visible: patterns should scale to hundreds of articles and experiments.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid motion that gates content visibility, and keep typography readable for long-form technical content across desktop and mobile.
